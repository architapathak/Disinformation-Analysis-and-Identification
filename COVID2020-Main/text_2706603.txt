For a complete list of diary events in Italy please click on . ISTAT releases January unemployment data (0900 GMT). Italy posted a state sector budget deficit of 1.6 billion euros in February, down steeply compared to a 9.765 billion euro deficit in the same month of 2019, due to an increase in tax revenues, the Treasury said on Monday. Moody’s downgraded the ratings of Atlantia and its motorway unit Autostrade to Ba3 with negative outlook. The downgrade reflects increasing political pressure and downside risks after a decree retroactively and unilaterally changing the terms and conditions of toll road concessions. It also reflects the apparent lack of progress in discussions linked to the future of the Autostrade concession. Long legal battles could increase uncertainty. A group of local shareholders, holding 8.6% of Italian bank UBI, is poised to reject Intesa Sanpaolo’s offer, Il Sole 24 Ore reported, bringing those who are against the takeover to almost 30% of the UBI capital. The three shareholders groups opposing Intesa Sanpaolo offer aim at achieving a 33% stake in UBI, Il Messaggero reported. Board meeting to approve new strategic plan, followed by presentation. Italy’s Banco BPM BAMI.MI is rushing to set up new “disaster recovery rooms” after an area close to one of its existing crisis facilities emerged as the epicentre of the coronavirus outbreak, three sources familiar with the matter said. New car sales in Italy fell 8.8% in February from a year earlier to 162.793 vehicles, the transport ministry said on Monday. Fiat Chrysler sales were down 6.7%. Industry Minister Stefano Patuanelli told Il Sole 24 Ore that the automotive sector needs new government incentives. Pirelli said its 2019 net profit rose to 457.7 million euros. It proposed a dividend of 0.183 euros.
