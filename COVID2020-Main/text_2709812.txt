LONDON, March 6 (Reuters) - A key gauge of long-term euro zone inflation expectations slid to a record low on Friday, reflecting entrenched pessimism over the economic outlook in the face of the coronavirus outbreak. The five-year, five-year breakeven inflation forward, an inflation gauge tracked by the European Central bank, slid to 1.0327%. The ECB targets inflation at close to 2%.
